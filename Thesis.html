Memristors & Reconfigurable Analogue Filters (Thesis)

What are Memristors?

Memristors have the following properties:

  • Fundamental, Two terminal devices
  • Relates flux and charge: d φ = M ⋅ dq
  • Passive: P(t) = I(t) ⋅ V(t) = I2(t)⋅ M(q(t))
  • Analogue - Time dependent, time varying
  • Non-linear
  • Stimuli dependent - the present state depends on the previous state
Figure on the Right: Illustration showing the relationships between the fundamental circuit measurement parameters and the fundamental circuit components. The fourth fundamental component is claimed to be the memristor.

How do they operate?

Short pulses across the device causes the boundary between the doped layers to move and thus the memristance to change.
